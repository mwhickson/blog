Well, it's official!

I have a blog using Hugo!

Time for a quick 'n' dirty collection of thoughts on the process thus far.

<!--more-->

Ultimately, there's not much to say.

Hugo does the job, and does it reasonably well.

If I were doing something that utilized more of the Hugo feature set, or I was more interested in building templates, or I was doing something more complex, then I might have hit upon more software 'warts'.

Setup, theme installation and content generation have thus far been reasonably easy. Sure, there's some magic incantations to ferret out and use, but nothing a mixture of consulting the documentation and a broader web search couldn't resolve reasonably quickly.

I like the fact that there's not too much cruft and boilerplate in a Hugo project. There's no denying that the software could have a smaller footprint; however, I'm not sure how much functionality would be lost as a result.

Things I still want to do with the blog include RSS (that's a bit of a sore spot), better content listing (for posts and lists), and possibly some general site restructing; however, the first hurdle is passed and I'm okay with where things sit.

My takeaway at this point is that Hugo is just fine for a personal blog, and probably has the legs to do something signficantly more involved.

In my case, that's good enough!

I can collect links, and share thoughts...

Mission accomplished!
